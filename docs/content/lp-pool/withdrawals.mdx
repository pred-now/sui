# Withdrawals and Cooldown

You can take capital out of the pool, but not capital that is currently backing
open risk. If the pool could be drained while it is backing live positions, the
capital rule would break and the whole system would be undercollateralized. So
withdrawals are gated.

## What is free to withdraw

At any moment, part of the pool is required to back open risk and the rest is free.

```ts
requiredCapital = 0.14 * leveragedOI
freeCapital     = poolAssets - requiredCapital
```

A withdrawal can take at most the free capital, and at most the hot slice actually
on hand. New leveraged positions are refused when they would push open interest
above `poolAssets / 0.14`. Withdrawals and new opens are gated on the same rule,
from both sides.

## Request, cooldown, claim

Unstaking is not instant. It runs in three steps: you **request** a withdrawal,
wait out a **cooldown** (an epoch or a fixed window), then **claim** the funds.

The cooldown exists to stop a bank run. Without it, stakers could rush the exit
exactly when stress is rising, draining the pool at the worst possible moment,
right when it is most needed to back risk.

## Re-checked at claim time

The utilization gate is checked again when you claim, not only when you request. If
the pool is fully utilized at claim time, so paying you would breach the capital
rule, the claim waits until there is enough free capital. This keeps the rule true
at every moment, not just on average.

## In short

- You can always withdraw your free capital.
- Capital backing open risk is locked until that risk clears.
- A cooldown sits between request and claim.
- The rule is enforced again at claim, so a payout never breaks solvency.
