# Staking and NAV

The pool uses standard vault accounting, the same shape as an ERC-4626 vault.

## Shares

When you stake, you receive **shares** of the pool. Shares are your claim on a
slice of the pool's assets. You do not get a separate stream of rewards. Your
shares simply become worth more as the pool earns.

The price of a share is the pool's assets divided by the total shares:

```
sharePrice = poolAssets / totalShares
```

Staking and unstaking convert between USDC and shares at that price:

```
deposit(amount):   shares  = amount * totalShares / poolAssets
withdraw(shares):  amount  = shares * poolAssets / totalShares
```

The very first deposit mints shares one to one. After that, every deposit and
withdrawal uses the live share price, so nobody can stake or unstake at a stale
valuation.

## NAV, the value of a share

NAV stands for net asset value, the value behind each share. You earn by NAV
going up, not by tokens being handed to you.

- A fee lands. `poolAssets` rises. Your share price rises.
- A loss lands. `poolAssets` falls. Your share price falls.

That is the whole mechanism. No emissions, no inflation, no rewards token.
Just the pool's real assets divided by the real shares.

## A quick example

Suppose the pool holds 1,000,000 USDC and has 1,000,000 shares. The share price
is 1.00.

- You stake 100,000 USDC and receive 100,000 shares. The pool now holds
  1,100,000 USDC and 1,100,000 shares, price still 1.00.
- Over time the pool earns 50,000 USDC in fees. Assets rise to 1,150,000, shares
  stay at 1,100,000. Share price is 1,150,000 / 1,100,000 = about 1.045.
- Your 100,000 shares are now worth about 104,500 USDC.

You did nothing. The shares gained value as the pool earned.

## The accounting always closes

The sum of every staker's share value equals `poolAssets`. No path mints or
burns shares without moving assets to match. This is one of the pool's
[invariants](/technical/capital-rule), checked continuously.
