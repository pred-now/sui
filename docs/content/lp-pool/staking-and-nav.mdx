# Staking and NAV

The pool uses standard vault accounting, the same shape as an ERC-4626 vault.

## Shares

When you stake, you receive shares of the pool. Shares are your claim on a slice
of the pool's assets. You do not get a separate stream of rewards. Your shares
simply become worth more as the pool earns.

The price of a share is the pool's assets divided by the total shares:

```ts
sharePrice = poolAssets / totalShares
```

Staking and unstaking convert between USDC and shares at that price:

```ts
deposit(amount):   shares = amount * totalShares / poolAssets
withdraw(shares):  amount = shares * poolAssets / totalShares
```

The first deposit ever mints shares one to one. After that, every deposit and
withdrawal uses the live share price, so nobody can stake or unstake at a stale
valuation.

## NAV, the value of a share

NAV stands for net asset value, the value behind each share. You earn by NAV going
up, not by tokens being handed to you. A fee raises `poolAssets`, so the share
price rises. A loss lowers `poolAssets`, so the share price falls.

That is the whole mechanism. No emissions, no inflation, no rewards token. Just
the pool's real assets divided by the real shares.

## A worked example

Suppose the pool holds 1,000,000 USDC and has 1,000,000 shares, so the share price
is 1.00.

```ts
// you stake 100,000
poolAssets  = 1,100,000
totalShares = 1,100,000
sharePrice  = 1.00          // still 1.00

// the pool earns 50,000 in fees
poolAssets  = 1,150,000
totalShares = 1,100,000
sharePrice  = 1.045         // 1,150,000 / 1,100,000

// your 100,000 shares are now worth
value = 100,000 * 1.045     // about 104,500
```

You did nothing. Your shares gained value as the pool earned.

## The accounting always closes

The sum of every staker's share value equals `poolAssets`. No path mints or burns
shares without moving assets to match. This is one of the pool's
[invariants](/technical/capital-rule), checked continuously.
