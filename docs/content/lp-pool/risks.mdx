# Risks

Staking in the LP pool is not a savings account. You earn real yield because you
take real risk. Here is the honest list.

<Callout type="warning" emoji="⚠️">
Pred is on testnet with test funds and the economy is still being tuned. Do not
treat any of this as a finished, audited product yet.
</Callout>

## You bear the tail

The pool's whole job is to stand behind losses. When a liquidation falls short,
or a position goes bad inside the leverage window, or a black swan hits, the pool
covers it and `poolAssets` drops. Every staker takes that hit pro rata through a
lower share price.

The design works hard to keep this small: pairing, steering, the 20 percent cap,
the deleverage cliff, and the 14 cent capital ratio. But small is not zero. A
rare black swan can still take a single thin pool down a noticeable amount.

## Correlated markets

Markets that resolve on the same real-world event jump together. They do not
diversify, so a group of them can lose at once. Pred treats a correlated group as
a single risk for the capital rule, gating on the combined open interest rather
than each market alone. Still, correlation is the risk most likely to cause an
outsized loss.

## Your capital can be locked

When the pool is backing open risk, the capital behind that risk cannot be
withdrawn until the risk clears. Add the unstake cooldown, and there are times
when you cannot exit immediately. See
[Withdrawals and Cooldown](/lp-pool/withdrawals).

## The economy is experimental

The fee split, the staker share, the cooldown length, and even the capital ratio
can change. These numbers come from simulation and will be re-tuned against real
flow. A parameter that looks safe today can be adjusted as we learn.

## What protects you

None of this is unmanaged. The same machinery that earns the yield also caps the
downside:

- The 20 percent imbalance cap holds losses below the break point.
- The 14 cent ratio sizes the pool to a conservative worst case.
- The hot slice is sized to survive a bad stretch on its own.
- Every fee and loss is counted exactly once, and balances are reconciled
  against the chain continuously.

The full safety argument is in [The Safety Wall](/economy/safety-wall) and
[The Capital Rule](/technical/capital-rule).
