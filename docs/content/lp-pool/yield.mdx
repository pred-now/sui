# Idle Yield

Most of the pool sits idle most of the time, waiting to back a loss that may never
come. Idle capital can earn a little extra without taking on new risk.

## Hot and supplied

The pool is split into two buckets.

```ts
hot      = liquid USDC Pred holds, backs current risk and redemptions
supplied = USDC supplied to a DeepBook Margin pool, earning lending yield
```

The interest earned on the supplied bucket accrues into `poolAssets`, so it flows
to stakers like any other revenue.

## Water marks

Funds move between the two buckets on simple thresholds.

```ts
if hot < lowWater:   pull funds from Margin into hot
if hot > highWater:  supply the excess to Margin
```

The hot bucket is sized to survive a bad stretch entirely on its own. The supplied
bucket is bonus yield and a slow top up, never a lifeline. Pred never relies on a
Margin withdrawal landing in time to cover a loss.

## Supply only, never borrow

> Pred only ever supplies and withdraws its own funds in DeepBook Margin. There is
> no borrow path. The pool never leverages itself.

## Mainnet only

This feature is mainnet only. On testnet the Predict quote asset is DUSDC, a mock
with no Margin pool to supply to, so idle yield is switched off there. Everything
else in the pool works the same on testnet, just without this extra slice.
