# Architecture

This section is the engineering detail behind the plain-English economy. If you
have not read [How the Economy Works](/economy/overview) yet, start there. The
source of truth for the numbers is the `PRED_ECON_ADVANCED` spec. Fixed-point
values are scaled by 1e9 unless stated, and prices and probabilities live in
the range 0 to 1.

## The engine is the accounting authority

Pred is custodial, and that is the key architectural decision. The chain holds
funds and positions. The **engine**, Pred's backend, holds everything else: the
bet imbalance, the pool, open interest, the borrow-rate state, and the ledger.

Because the engine is the single authority, steering, netting, and every gate
are in-memory reads against engine state. Decisions are made before any
transaction is signed. The chain is where decisions are committed, not where
they are computed.

## Single writer per market

Every mutation to a market is serialized by **one writer** for that market.

<Callout type="info">
A single writer per market means no two opens on the same market race on chain.
They are ordered in the engine first, then each becomes one signed transaction.
This sidesteps Sui shared-object contention entirely.
</Callout>

It also matters for correctness. Steering must read a consistent snapshot of the
imbalance to make the right decision. A single writer gives that snapshot for
free. A per-user lock separately serializes withdrawals against liquidation, so
a user cannot withdraw equity that a pending liquidation is about to consume.

## State

The engine holds, per market:

- Bet imbalance, signed.
- Leveraged long notional `L` and short notional `S`.
- Escrow, pool, and peak open interest.
- The correlation cohort id.
- The latest oracle reference.

And per user: collateral, debt, accrued fee, and open positions.

## Idempotency

Every external effect is idempotent, keyed on an id:

- Deposits key on the funding transaction digest.
- Opens, closes, liquidations, and withdrawals key on a request id.

So a retry after an ambiguous chain result settles exactly once. The engine
ledger is the truth. A reconciliation job continuously checks engine balances
against on-chain balances and alarms on any drift.

## The two engines

Pred runs two risk engines with separate state:

- The **house engine** prices and steers bets. See
  [Pricing](/technical/pricing) and [Steering](/technical/steering).
- The **leverage engine** prices and steers loans, and handles
  [netting](/technical/netting), [liquidation](/technical/liquidation), and the
  [capital rule](/technical/capital-rule).

They share one number where it matters: the pool, read by both through the 0.14
capital gate.
