# Netting and Residual

Netting is the cheap-hedge core of both engines. The idea is the same for bets
and for loans: matched opposite flow covers itself, and only the leftover needs
real work.

## Match first, route the residual

The house engine internalizes matched YES and NO bets. A matched pair earns the
full `2h` overround and never touches the vault. Only the net imbalance is laid
off to the DeepBook Predict vault at the vault spread.

Per market Pred tracks `internalYes`, `internalNo`, and `hedgedToVault`. Net
exposure is:

```
net = internalYes - internalNo - hedgedToVault
```

When `net` crosses the band, Pred mints the offsetting side from the vault to
pull it back. Above the hard cap, the engine refuses opens that increase
exposure and accepts only balancing ones. That refusal is an in-memory reject,
before any transaction is signed.

## Netting the leverage book

Matched leveraged longs and shorts collateralize each other. In every outcome
the winning side repays its own loan, and the losing side's collateral covers
its own loan.

> Up to `min(L, S)`, the loans are never at risk and Pred buys nothing. Only the
> naked `|L - S|` needs a hedge or the pool.

## Netting is a view, not a transfer

Pred does not move anyone's loan. Netting is an accounting view. The solvency
math simply keys off `|L - S|`. So pool sizing, hedge sizing, and the
[capital gate](/technical/capital-rule) all read the naked imbalance, never the
gross. One number per market does the work.

This is why the whole design stays cheap: the gross book can be large, but the
only thing that consumes capital is the small naked leftover that netting and
[steering](/technical/steering) could not remove.
