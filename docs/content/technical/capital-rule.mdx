# The Capital Rule

Two hard limits make the whole design safe: the imbalance ceiling and the
capital rule. Both are enforced before an open is accepted, not as a setup
calculation.

## Where 0.14 comes from

Pred simulated the worst pool loss per unit of peak leveraged open interest,
over 20,000 single-market runs including black swans.

| loss / OI | p99 | p99.9 | p99.99 |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| normal | 0.070 | 0.110 | 0.148 |
| jump | 0.085 | 0.136 | 0.166 |

The rule takes the conservative p99.9:

> Hold pool plus treasury at least **0.14 times** peak leveraged open interest.
> Equivalently, cap leveraged OI at about **7 times** the pool.

In simulation, sizing the pool to 0.14 times median OI drops single-market
insolvency from about 0.5 percent to about 0.04 percent.

## The admission check

Every open is checked in the engine before any signing:

```
projImb = imbalance after this open
projOI  = peak leveraged OI for the backing pool after this open

accept if projImb <= ceiling AND projOI <= pool / 0.14
else accept only if the open reduces imbalance, otherwise refuse
```

The ceiling is 20 percent, well below the roughly 34 percent break point. See
[The Safety Wall](/economy/safety-wall) for the three-layer margin.

## Correlation cohorts

One subtlety. The 0.14 ratio was derived from single-market tails. Markets that
resolve on the same real-world event jump together and do not diversify.

<Callout type="warning">
For a group of correlated markets backed by one pool, gate on the summed cohort
open interest, not per market. Independent, staggered markets can share a pool
with a smaller aggregate buffer because their tails are independent.
</Callout>

So each market is tagged with a correlation cohort id. The gate sums OI within a
cohort and applies 0.14 to that sum. A market defaults into its own cohort
unless explicitly marked independent. Conservative by default, which is what you
want for the rule that keeps Pred solvent.

## The invariants

These hold at all times, checked continuously:

- `poolAssets >= 0.14 * leveragedOI`. Both new opens and withdrawals are gated
  on it.
- `hot >=` the floor needed to cover a bad stretch without waiting on a Margin
  withdrawal.
- Share accounting closes: the sum of all share values equals `poolAssets`. No
  path mints or burns shares without moving assets.
- Pred only ever supplies and withdraws its own funds in Margin. No borrow path
  exists.
- Every fee and every loss moves `poolAssets` exactly once, keyed on the
  originating event id, so a retry never double counts.

These are not aspirations. They are re-proven by the
[validation harness](/technical/harness) on every parameter change.
