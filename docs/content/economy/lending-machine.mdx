# The Lending Machine

The lending machine lets people bet bigger with borrowed money, and keeps Pred
from going broke when they lose.

## Why lending on a bet is hard

Two reasons, and both are real.

1. **The payoff is tilted against the lender.** When the borrower wins, they keep
   the upside and Pred just collects its interest. When the borrower loses, Pred
   can be left holding an unpaid loan. Capped gain, uncapped loss.
2. **A bet can vanish in an instant.** At resolution the price jumps to 1 or 0.
   There is no smooth slide to unwind a loan along.

A model built for ordinary, slidable assets does not cover this. So the lending
machine is its own engine, separate from the betting machine.

## The four moves

### 1. Let opposite borrowers cover each other

A user borrowing to bet big on YES and a user borrowing to bet big on NO cancel
out, just like in the betting machine. Whoever wins repays their own loan, and
the loser's deposit covers theirs. For every matched pair, Pred lends safely and
spends nothing. Only the naked imbalance between the two sides needs covering.

### 2. Nudge the borrow rate

If too many people borrow to bet YES, Pred makes borrowing for YES cost more and
borrowing for NO cost less. Leveraged traders are rate sensitive, so they
flatten the imbalance themselves. This is the **main safety dial**, named
`dynamicK`.

The balancing side can even get a rebate. That rebate is capped at what Pred
saves by not having to hedge that unit, so a balancing borrower is never
subsidized beyond the value they provide.

### 3. Keep a small safety pool

Whatever leftover imbalance the nudging does not clear is covered two ways: by
buying the opposite side from the vault, and by a small pool funded out of the
interest borrowers pay. Because it only ever covers the leftover, the pool stays
small. This pool is funded by stakers, covered in [The LP Pool](/lp-pool/overview).

### 4. Take the borrowed money off the table before the jump

In the last minutes before a market resolves, Pred closes the borrowed part of
every position. The user keeps their own bet, just not the borrowed money on
top.

<Callout type="info">
This is the deleverage cliff. After it runs, debt is zero everywhere, so the
resolution jump lands only on ordinary unleveraged positions. The jump can never
create a bad debt. This single move is what converts unmanageable jump risk into
ordinary, manageable risk.
</Callout>

## Putting it together

Netting handles the matched part for free. Steering shrinks the leftover.
The pool and a hedge cover what remains. The cliff removes the jump. Each move
takes a bite out of the risk, and what is left for real capital to absorb is
small.
