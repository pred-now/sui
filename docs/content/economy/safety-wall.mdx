# The Safety Wall

The nudging keeps the book balanced most of the time. The wall is what keeps
Pred safe the rest of the time.

## A hard cap on imbalance

Pred never lets one side get more than a set amount lopsided. As the book leans
toward that cap, the nudging gets stronger and stronger. Past the cap, Pred
simply refuses new bets on the crowded side and accepts only balancing ones.

The cap sits at **20 percent** imbalance. The point where the safety pool would
actually start to fail is around **34 percent**. So the cap is set well below the
danger line, on purpose.

## The three layers

The design holds because each layer sits comfortably below the next.

| Layer | Level | Role |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Normal flow | ~11% | Where steering holds the book day to day |
| Soft ceiling | 20% | The hard cap, dormant in normal operation |
| Break point | ~34% | Where the pool would start to fail |

The gap between each layer is the room Pred has to be slightly wrong about any
single number and still be fine.

## The most important property

Here is the part worth internalizing.

<Callout type="warning" emoji="🔑">
**Pred stays solvent even if nobody responds to the nudge.**

Because the 20 percent cap holds the imbalance below the break no matter who
flips, insolvency stays near zero across every level of crowd responsiveness we
tested. What changes with responsiveness is the rejection rate, not the solvency
rate.
</Callout>

In plain terms: if traders ignore the borrow-rate nudge, Pred just has to turn
away more bets to keep the book under the cap. That costs Pred some business. It
does not cost the pool. A wrong guess about how people respond is a user
experience problem we can see and fix live, never a solvency problem.

## How much capital to hold

One simple ratio sets it.

> Hold at least **14 cents** of safety capital for every dollar of leveraged
> open interest. Said the other way: lend up to about **7 times** the size of
> the pool.

When borrowing approaches that limit, Pred stops accepting new leveraged opens
until the pool grows. This is not a one-time setup calculation. It is enforced
continuously as a live invariant, using the same wall machinery applied to open
interest.

The 0.14 figure comes from simulating the worst pool loss per dollar of open
interest across tens of thousands of runs, including black swans, and taking a
conservative high percentile. The derivation is in
[The Capital Rule](/technical/capital-rule).

## What can still go wrong

- **Informed flow.** Priced by the fast-flow fee and bounded by the cap. Not
  removed, only priced and capped.
- **Correlation.** Markets resolving on the same event jump together and do not
  diversify. Pred keeps leveraged markets on independent, staggered deadlines,
  or holds deeper capital for a correlated group.
- **A rare black swan.** It can still nick a single thin pool now and then. A
  deeper reserve absorbs it, and the 14 cent ratio sets how deep.
