# The Two Pots of Money

It helps to keep two pots clearly separate in your head. One holds user money and
pays the winners. The other holds Pred's money and backs the risk.

## Pot one: user money

Every market has a pot of user funds that pays the winners. When a YES bettor and
a NO bettor are paired, the loser's stake pays the winner. When a bet is hedged at
the vault, that hedge pays for itself. This pot is self-funding by design. Pred is
just holding the jar.

## Pot two: Pred's money

Pred's own capital backs only two things:

- The carried bet imbalance, the leftover the betting machine chooses to hold
  rather than hedge.
- The leveraged positions, the leftover the lending machine could not net or
  steer away.

This pot moves up and down a little. It comes out ahead on average, because the
wall and the 14 cent ratio keep the downside small while volume keeps the average
steady.

## Why the average is reliable

> A casino with one table can have a bad night. A casino with a thousand tables
> basically cannot.

The carried imbalance is a fair, zero expected value bet. On any single market it
can win or lose. Across many independent markets the wins and losses average out.
This is why Pred never concentrates its capital on one market or one event, and
why correlated markets, which fail to diversify, are treated with extra care.

## Where the LP pool fits

Pot two, Pred's risk capital, does not have to be Pred's own money. Outside
stakers can fund it and earn the fees it generates. That is the LP pool, and it is
the same capital described here: the premium pool behind the leverage band and the
buffer behind the carried imbalance.
