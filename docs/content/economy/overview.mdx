# How the Economy Works

Pred does two jobs, so it helps to think of it as two machines that run side by
side.

- **The betting machine** keeps Pred neutral on outcomes. It handles betting.
- **The lending machine** keeps Pred solvent when borrowers lose. It handles
  leverage.

They are separate on purpose, because they manage different risks. But they
share the same playbook:

1. **Pair people up for free.** A YES and a NO cancel out. So do a leveraged
   long and a leveraged short. Matched flow costs Pred nothing.
2. **Nudge to stay balanced.** Pred changes prices and borrow rates so the crowd
   itself flattens the leftover. The nudge is free.
3. **Cover the small leftover.** Whatever is left after pairing and nudging is
   hedged or backed by a pool. Because it is only the leftover, it is small.
4. **Put a wall in front of the worst case.** A hard cap on how lopsided a market
   can get means Pred stays safe even if nobody responds to the nudge.

## Why this shape matters

The expensive part of being a bookmaker is the imbalance, the side of the book
with more money on it and nobody on the other side. Everything Pred does is
aimed at keeping that imbalance tiny:

- Pairing removes it for free.
- Nudging shrinks what is left.
- The wall caps what nudging cannot clear.

Only the final sliver ever needs real capital behind it. That is why Pred can
run on a surprisingly small amount of risk money relative to the volume it
handles.
