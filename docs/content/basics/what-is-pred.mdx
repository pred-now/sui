# What is Pred

Pred is a place to bet on yes-or-no questions about the real world.

Each market is a single question with two sides, YES and NO. For example,
"Will BTC close above 100k on Friday?" You buy the side you believe in. When
the question resolves, the winning side gets paid and the losing side does not.

## Pred is the bookmaker

Picture someone holding a jar. One person bets YES and puts money in. Another
person bets NO and puts money in. The winner takes the jar. Pred keeps a small
slice for running the table.

That is the whole idea. Pred is the house. It quotes prices, takes both sides,
and pays the winner with the loser's money. **Pred never bets on an outcome
itself.** It does not care whether YES or NO wins, only that it collects its
slice on the way through.

## A quick example

Say the fair odds of YES are 40 cents on the dollar.

- Pred sells YES for 41 cents.
- Pred sells NO for 61 cents.

One person buys YES, one buys NO. That is 41 + 61 = 102 cents collected. The
winner is paid 100 cents. Pred keeps 2 cents. It does not matter who wins. The
profit was locked in the moment both bets were placed.

That 2 cent margin is called the **spread**. It is how the bookmaker earns.

## What you need to know to start

- A market is a yes-or-no question with a deadline.
- A price between 0 and 1 is the market's view of the odds. 65 cents means
  roughly a 65 percent chance.
- You buy YES or NO at the price Pred quotes.
- When the market resolves, a winning share pays out 1 dollar, a losing share
  pays 0.

That is enough to place your first bet. The next page explains where the prices
come from.
