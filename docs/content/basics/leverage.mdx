# Using Leverage

Leverage lets you make a bigger bet than your own cash would allow. You put up
some money, Pred lends you the rest, and your position acts larger than your
deposit.

## A simple example

You have 100 dollars and you are confident about YES. With 3x leverage, you
control a 300 dollar position. You put in 100, Pred lends 200.

- If YES wins, you keep the gains on the full 300, then repay the 200 plus a
  little interest. Your upside is much bigger.
- If YES loses, the losses come out of your 100 first. Lose enough and your
  position is closed to protect the loan.

Pred currently allows up to **5x** leverage. There are no separate per-user
caps.

## Interest, called the borrow rate

Borrowing is not free. You pay a borrow rate while the loan is open. The rate is
not fixed. It moves with how lopsided the market is:

- If lots of people are borrowing to bet the same side, joining that side costs
  more.
- Borrowing to bet the other side costs less, sometimes even earns a rebate.

This nudge keeps the two sides roughly balanced, which is what keeps lending
safe. More on this in [The Lending Machine](/economy/lending-machine).

## Liquidation

If your position loses enough value, it gets **liquidated**: closed early to make
sure the loan is repaid. Liquidation happens at around 7 percent remaining
equity. Whatever is left after repaying the loan and fees is returned to you.

Pred does not liquidate on a hair trigger. A position only becomes liquidatable
30 seconds after the oracle update that crossed the line, and liquidation pauses
if a second price source disagrees with the oracle. This blocks a single bad
print from wiping you out.

## The deleverage cliff

Here is the part that makes leverage on a prediction market work at all.

A normal market can fall step by step, so a loan can be wound down smoothly. A
prediction market does not. At resolution it **jumps**: YES goes straight to 1
or straight to 0, with no path in between. A loan caught in that jump could
blow straight through its collateral.

So Pred closes the borrowed part of every position in the final minutes before
resolution. This is the **deleverage cliff**.

<Callout type="info">
After the cliff, your debt is zero. You still hold your own bet, just without
the borrowed money on top. The resolution jump then lands on an ordinary,
unleveraged position, where it cannot create a bad debt.
</Callout>

You do not have to do anything. The cliff runs automatically per market just
before its deadline.

## The short version

- You put up part, Pred lends the rest, up to 5x.
- You pay a borrow rate that leans to keep the book balanced.
- Lose too much and you are liquidated at about 7 percent equity.
- Just before resolution, the borrowed part is closed for you, so the final
  jump never hits a loan.

Why all of this is safe even in the worst case is the subject of
[The Safety Wall](/economy/safety-wall).
